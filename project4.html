Public Health England: Regression

Finding the best regression approach for predicting cancer deaths in UK communities.

Objective. Using public health data from across England, see if deaths from cancer in a region can be somewhat accurately predicted using a variety of health and environmental markers.

Approach. I accessed Public Health England's 'Fingertips' dataset via its API (using the custom Python library fingertips_py) and selected 45 health and environmental markers across 299 regions of the country. The markers included indicators of noise level, indoor air quality, education level, pollution, rates of obesity and sexually-transmitted diseases, etc. My goal was two-fold:

  1. To determine if it is indeed possible to predict terminal cancer rates with some accuracy based on these kinds of community characteristics.
  2. To identify any specific features that have strong explanatory power and may thus be explored as potential contributing factors to cancer deaths.

I used several different approaches: linear regression, Elastic Net, and LASSO to hone in key features, as well as a decision tree to understand the explanatory power of those features; and then AdaBoost and PCA to build a model with more predictive strength.
